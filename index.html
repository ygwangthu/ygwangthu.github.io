Opening PhD and Master Positions
Yangang Wang is currently a full professor at Southeast University (SEU), affiliated with both the School of Automation and the School of Future Technology. Before joining SEU, he worked as a research scientist at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) from 2014 to 2017. He received his Ph.D. in 2014 from the Department of Automation at Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Qionghai Dai. His primary research areas are Computer Vision, Computer Graphics and Virtual Reality. His most recent research interests are digital virtual human (DVH) and dynamic shape reconstruction with single or multiview cameras (a.k.a. markerless motion capture), including
  • 3D/4D human capture, modeling and reconstruction
  • Hand pose acquisition, modeling and simulation
  • Body shape reconstruction, simulation and control
  • Tiny motion or vibration sensing, measurement and analysis
He serves as an area chair for ACM MM2024-2025 and has been a program committee member of AAAI2019-2022, Chinagraph2020-2022 and CICAI2022. He is also a reviewer for journals and conferences such as Nature Computational Science / TVCG / TPAMI / SIGGRAPH / CVPR / ICCV / NeurIPS, etc.
